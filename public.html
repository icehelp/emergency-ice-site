ICE Is in Public or at Work

Stop. Breathe. Stay calm.
Do not run. Do not argue. Keep your hands visible.

First question

“Am I free to leave?”

If yes: walk away calmly. If no: stay silent and ask for a lawyer.

Say this (out loud)

“I am exercising my right to remain silent.”
“I do not consent to any searches.”
“I want to speak to a lawyer.”

Do not: answer questions about immigration status or where you were born, hand over documents unless legally required, or physically interfere.

Documents

If you are driving, you may be required to show your driver’s license/registration if demanded by police. Otherwise, you do not have to hand over documents just because ICE asks. If unsure, state that you want to speak to a lawyer.

If you are a witness or bystander

  • Record from a safe distance if it is safe
  • Do not block officers or interfere physically
  • Note time, location, vehicles, badge numbers (if visible)

Social media and accountability

If no one is in immediate danger and it is safe, sharing verified information publicly can increase accountability. Avoid live posting if it could escalate the situation. Prioritize safety and accuracy.
