ICE Is at My Door

Stop. Breathe. Stay calm.
If possible, put children and pets in a separate room and keep everyone away from the door.

DO NOT OPEN THE DOOR

Do not open the door. Do not let anyone in. Speak through the door.

Say this (out loud)

“I do not consent to you entering my home.
Please show me a warrant signed by a judge.”
“I am exercising my right to remain silent.
I want to speak to a lawyer.”

Do not: do not answer questions, do not sign anything, do not physically interfere.

Important to know

ICE may say many things to get you to open the door or come outside. You can keep the door closed. You have the right to remain silent.

Warrants — keep this simple

  • A judge-signed warrant is signed by a judge and often references a court (e.g., U.S. District Court).
  • An ICE administrative warrant (DHS/ICE paperwork) is generally not enough to enter your home without consent.

If they claim to have a warrant, ask them to slide it under the door or hold it up to a window. Check for a judge’s signature and the correct address.

If they enter anyway

Do not physically resist.

“I do not consent to your entry or search.
I am exercising my right to remain silent.
I want to speak to a lawyer.”

If it is safe, record audio/video and note the time and what is happening.

After the interaction

  • Write down names, badge numbers, and agency (if visible)
  • Save photos/videos
  • Write down exactly what was said
  • Contact legal help

Sharing information

If it is safe and the situation has ended, you may choose to share verified information publicly to increase accountability. Do not post in real time if it could escalate the situation or put anyone at risk. Share facts only.

DO NOT

  • Do not open the door unless shown a judge-signed warrant
  • Do not lie or guess answers
  • Do not sign anything without a lawyer
  • Do not physically interfere or resist
