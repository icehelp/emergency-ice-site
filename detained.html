If Someone Is Being Detained

Stop. Breathe. Stay calm.
Your job is to gather information and get legal help.

Do this first

  • Write down full name, date of birth, and where it happened
  • Ask where they are being taken (if safe)
  • Document time, location, vehicles, badges (if visible)
  • Call for legal help as soon as possible

Do not: let them sign anything without a lawyer; do not physically interfere; do not post private details publicly without consent.

Immediate checklist

  • Full name (exact spelling)
  • Date of birth
  • Country of birth
  • A-number (if known)
  • Where and when they were taken
  • Which agency was involved
  • Any documents given or shown

Do NOT let them sign anything

Do not allow the person to sign forms, statements, or “voluntary departure” paperwork without a lawyer.

How to post bond (quick guide)

If the person is eligible for release on bond, ICE (or an immigration judge) may set a dollar amount. To post bond, someone on the outside pays the full amount and receives a receipt.

  • Confirm bond amount: Ask for the person’s A-number and the bond amount, if set.
  • Pay in full: Bonds are generally paid in full (no payment plans).
  • Online option: ICE uses an official system called CeBONDS and payments are typically by bank transfer (ACH/Fedwire).
  • In-person option: Some bond acceptance offices take in-person payment (usually cashier’s/certified check). Call ahead for hours and requirements.
  • Avoid scams: ICE bonds are not paid via Zelle, Western Union, etc.
  • If denied or too high: A lawyer may request a bond hearing before an immigration judge.

Where ICE holds people (NYC / NJ)

People detained by ICE in the NYC/NJ area are often held temporarily and may be transferred.

  • New York: 26 Federal Plaza (ICE ERO bond office), MDC Brooklyn, and sometimes upstate facilities.
  • New Jersey: Newark ICE Field Office (processing/enforcement), Elizabeth Detention Facility, and sometimes county jails under contract.

How immigration bonds are paid

Most bonds are paid online (CeBONDS via bank transfer), but in-person payment is still possible in some cases.

  • Online: You generally need the person’s full name, A-number, and bond amount. You do not need to go to a facility.
  • In person: Often by cashier’s or certified check payable to “Department of Homeland Security”. Call ahead to confirm hours and rules.

If you are unsure which option applies, call a 24/7 hotline on the Resources page first.

Public awareness

When appropriate and safe, sharing verified information publicly may help draw attention and support. Do not share private details without consent. Do not post if it could put anyone at risk or interfere with legal efforts.
